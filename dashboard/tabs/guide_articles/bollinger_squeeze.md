## Bollinger Band Squeeze

**In plain English:** Bollinger Bands normally expand and contract based on market volatility. When the bands get extremely narrow (a "squeeze"), it means the market has been unusually quiet — and quiet markets always precede volatile ones. The squeeze identifies the setup; you then wait for the breakout direction and trade it with momentum.

---

### The Squeeze Concept

Bollinger Bands = 20-day SMA ± 2 standard deviations. When volatility is very low, the bands narrow to their tightest range. Historically, the tighter the bands get, the more explosive the eventual breakout.

**Bandwidth formula:** (Upper BB − Lower BB) / Middle BB

When bandwidth drops to its lowest level in 6 months: **Squeeze alert.**

### The Two-Phase Entry

**Phase 1 — Squeeze identification:** Identify that bandwidth is at multi-month lows. This is just a setup — no trade yet.

**Phase 2 — Directional confirmation:** Wait for the first close OUTSIDE the bands on above-average volume AND with momentum indicator turning in the breakout direction. THEN enter. Missing Phase 2 means you're guessing direction, not following it.

---

### Entry Checklist

- [ ] Bollinger Band bandwidth at 6-month low (squeeze active)
- [ ] Price breaks OUTSIDE the band (close, not intraday spike)
- [ ] Volume > 1.3× 30-day average on breakout bar
- [ ] Momentum oscillator confirms direction (turns positive/negative)
- [ ] VIX low (< 20) — squeeze setups work better in low-vol environments

---

### Common Mistakes

1. **Anticipating the breakout direction.** The entire point is to WAIT for the market to tell you which way it breaks. Many traders pick a direction before the breakout and get trapped when it goes the other way.

2. **Entering on an intraday breakout that closes back inside the bands.** A wick outside the bands is not a squeeze breakout. The CLOSE must be outside the bands.

3. **Holding through the next squeeze.** After a squeeze breakout, the market trends for a while and then returns to consolidation. Exit when momentum indicators turn or after a new squeeze forms in the opposite direction.
