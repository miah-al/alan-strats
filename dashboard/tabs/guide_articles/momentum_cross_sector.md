## Cross-Sector Momentum

**In plain English:** Every month, rank all 11 S&P 500 sector ETFs by their 12-month return (excluding the most recent month). Go long the top 3 sectors and short the bottom 3. Strong sectors keep outperforming; weak sectors keep underperforming — this is one of the most robust anomalies in finance, documented since 1999.

---

### The 11 Sectors

XLK (Tech) · XLF (Finance) · XLE (Energy) · XLV (Healthcare) · XLY (Consumer Disc) · XLP (Staples) · XLI (Industrials) · XLB (Materials) · XLU (Utilities) · XLRE (Real Estate) · XLC (Communication)

### Why "12-1" and Not Just "12"?

The most recent month's return shows **short-term reversal** (overreaction mean-reversion). Including it would incorrectly penalize recent strong sectors and reward recent weak ones. Excluding the last month isolates the 2–12 month momentum that has genuine predictive power.

---

### Performance Context

From 1994 to 2024, cross-sector momentum has shown:
- Sharpe ratio: ~0.8–1.0 (gross of costs)
- Best years: Strong trending environments (2017, 2019, 2023)
- Worst years: Broad correlation crash — when ALL sectors fall together (2008, 2022), the long side doesn't help

---

### Common Mistakes

1. **Rebalancing too often.** Monthly is correct. Weekly rebalancing increases transaction costs and captures more noise than signal.

2. **Ignoring the "minus 1 month" rule.** Testing shows including the last month's return reduces Sharpe by 0.2–0.3. It's counterintuitive but the academic evidence is clear.

3. **Trading all 11 sectors equally.** The bottom 3 short positions often include defensive sectors (utilities, staples) that don't short well in bull markets. Consider reducing short side in strong bull regimes.
