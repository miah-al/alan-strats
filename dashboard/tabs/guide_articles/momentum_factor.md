## MA Crossover Momentum

**In plain English:** When the 20-day moving average crosses above the 50-day moving average, the short-term trend is accelerating upward — enter long. When it crosses below, exit or go short. This is the oldest systematic strategy in technical trading. Simple, clear rules, and decades of evidence that medium-term momentum is a real phenomenon.

---

### Why Moving Averages Work (The Real Reason)

It's not magic. Moving averages work because they're self-fulfilling: millions of traders and algorithms watch the same signals. When the 50-day MA is breached on high volume, systematic traders and algorithms buy. Their buying pushes prices higher. Other traders see the move and buy. This cascade creates the momentum effect.

The 20/50-day crossover is one of the most widely watched setups globally — which paradoxically gives it more power, not less.

### MA Crossover Types

| Crossover | Short MA | Long MA | Typical Hold | Use For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fast | 5-day | 20-day | Days | Swing trades, volatile markets |
| **Standard** | **20-day** | **50-day** | **Weeks** | **Core momentum signal** |
| Slow (Golden Cross) | 50-day | 200-day | Months | Long-term positioning |

The **20/50 crossover** is the balance — responsive enough to catch moves, slow enough to filter noise.

### Volume Confirmation is Critical

A crossover without volume is a weak signal. Compare:

**Strong crossover:**
- 20-day crosses 50-day
- Volume on crossover day: 2.5× 30-day average
- RSI(14) = 58 (momentum building, not overbought)
- Historical win rate with this setup: ~65%

**Weak crossover:**
- 20-day barely clips above 50-day (difference: $0.05)
- Volume: 0.8× average (below average — no conviction)
- RSI(14) = 72 (already overbought)
- Historical win rate with this setup: ~42% (barely above random)

### Entry Checklist

- [ ] 20-day MA crosses above 50-day MA (or below for short)
- [ ] Volume on crossover day ≥ 1.5× 30-day average
- [ ] RSI(14) between 45–65 (not overbought, has room to run)
- [ ] SPY above 200-day MA (for long entries) — trading in the primary trend direction
- [ ] No divergence in sector breadth (>60% of SPX sectors confirming the move)

### Common Mistakes

1. **Trading every crossover mechanically without volume confirmation.** In low-volume, rangebound markets, the 20/50 crossover whipsaws repeatedly. Volume is the difference between a real trend and a false signal.

2. **Shorting when SPY is above its 200-day MA.** The 200-day MA is the master trend filter. Short signals from the 20/50 crossover are only valid when the bigger trend is down. Shorting a pullback in a bull market is fighting the primary trend.

3. **Ignoring the golden cross / death cross context.** The 50/200-day crossover (golden cross = bullish, death cross = bearish) tells you the macro environment. If you're getting a bearish 20/50 crossover but the 50/200 is still bullish (golden cross), it's a counter-trend correction — be less aggressive on the short side.

4. **Using too short a lookback in volatile markets.** In 2022, the 20/50 MA crossover generated 8 signals in one year — 5 were whipsaws. A longer-period MA (30/65 or 50/100) would have filtered out some noise at the cost of later entries.
