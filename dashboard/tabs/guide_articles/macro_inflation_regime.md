## Macro Inflation Regime

**In plain English:** Inflation isn't just a number — it's a regime. Whether CPI is rising, falling, above target, or below target dramatically changes what asset classes work. This strategy defines four inflation regimes and maintains a different asset allocation for each: Goldilocks (low and falling), Reflation (low but rising), Stagflation (high and rising), and Disinflation (high but falling). Regime transitions trigger systematic rebalancing.

---

### The Four Inflation Regimes

The framework uses two variables: **CPI level** (above or below 3%) and **CPI direction** (rising or falling month-over-month):

| Regime | CPI Level | Direction | Economy | Best Assets |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Goldilocks** | < 3% | Falling | Growing | Equities (growth), bonds |
| **Reflation** | < 3% | Rising | Recovering | Equities (cyclicals), commodities |
| **Stagflation** | > 3% | Rising | Slowing | Commodities, TIPS, gold, energy |
| **Disinflation** | > 3% | Falling | Recovering | Equities (growth → value), bonds |

Most of 2010–2019 was Goldilocks. 2021–2022 was Reflation → Stagflation. 2023 was Disinflation.

---

### How to Measure and Track

**Primary signal:** Core CPI YoY (excludes food and energy, less volatile)

**Secondary signals:**
- PCE deflator (Fed's preferred measure — react to this before CPI when they diverge)
- 5-Year breakeven inflation rate (market-implied inflation expectation from TIPS spread)
- PPI (producer prices — leads CPI by 2–3 months)

**Regime trigger:**
- Level threshold: 3.0% for Core CPI
- Direction: positive if current month > 3-month moving average; negative if below

### Real Trade Walkthrough

> **January 2022 → December 2022: Reflation to Stagflation to Disinflation transition**

**January 2022 state:**
- Core CPI: 6.0% (above 3%) and rising → **Stagflation regime**
- Portfolio position: overweight energy (XLE 15%), gold (GLD 10%), short TLT (or underweight bonds), neutral equities

**March 2022 FOMC:** Fed begins hiking at 25 bps. Energy (XLE) +60% YTD by June 2022.

**June 2022 reading:** Core CPI peaked at 6.3% in September 2022; by October, sequential data shows deceleration.

**November 2022 signal:** CPI prints 7.1% (below expectations); 5-year breakevens fall from 3.0% to 2.4%. Direction turns negative even though level remains high → **Disinflation regime trigger**

**Rebalancing on Disinflation signal:**
- Reduce XLE from 15% to 5% (sell at ~$85, had entered ~$55 — locked in gains)
- Reduce GLD from 10% to 5%
- Buy TLT: shift from underweight to neutral (TLT at $97, near its lows)
- Add long-duration growth: initiate QQQ position (tech rallies in disinflation)

**Outcome Q1 2023:**
- QQQ: +20% (AI narrative + disinflation)
- TLT: +12% as rates fell
- XLE: +5% (oil stabilized)
- Overall portfolio: +14.2% in Q1 2023

### Asset Performance by Regime

**Goldilocks (Low CPI, falling):**
- US large-cap growth: +18% avg annual
- Bonds (TLT): +8% avg annual
- Gold: +3% (no inflation premium needed)
- Commodities: flat to negative
- **Strategy: standard 60/40, overweight growth stocks**

**Reflation (Low CPI, rising):**
- Cyclicals (XLI, XLB, XLE): +22% avg annual
- Small caps (IWM): +25% avg annual
- Value stocks: +15%
- Bonds: 0% to −5% (rates rising)
- **Strategy: rotate to cyclicals/value, underweight bonds and growth**

**Stagflation (High CPI, rising):**
- Energy (XLE): +35% avg annual
- Gold (GLD): +15%
- TIPS (TIP): +8%
- US equities (SPY): −5% to −10%
- Long bonds (TLT): −20% to −30%
- **Strategy: heavy commodities/energy, short duration, minimal equities**

**Disinflation (High CPI, falling):**
- US growth equities: +25% avg annual
- Long bonds (TLT): +15% as rates fall
- REITs: +12%
- Energy: moderate (oil peaks near stagflation → disinflation transition)
- **Strategy: rotate back to growth equities + long bonds, reduce commodities**

### Options Positioning by Regime

**Goldilocks:** Low VIX environment → optimal for selling premium (iron condors, covered calls). IV is low relative to realized vol.

**Reflation:** VIX moderately elevated (15–20); sell puts on cyclicals (they tend to rally), buy calls on commodity ETFs.

**Stagflation:** VIX elevated (20–30+), markets choppy. Reduce vol selling. Buy puts on rate-sensitive sectors. Consider VIX calls for additional hedging.

**Disinflation:** VIX normalizing from elevated levels. Transition period — add equity exposure gradually. Buy calls on QQQ on any pullback.

### Entry Checklist

- [ ] Check CPI release date (first two weeks of each month); update regime signal within 24 hours
- [ ] Confirm direction using 3-month trailing average of CPI, not just one month
- [ ] Check breakeven inflation (TIPS spread) as forward-looking confirmation
- [ ] Only rebalance when regime CHANGES — don't over-trade within a regime
- [ ] Weight transition trades over 2–4 weeks (don't flip all at once on one print)

---

### Common Mistakes

1. **Reacting to one CPI print.** One month's data is noise. CPI is revised, and seasonal adjustments can create false signals. Wait for 2–3 consecutive prints confirming the direction change before declaring a regime transition.

2. **Treating stagflation positions as permanent.** Energy and commodities are spectacular in stagflation but crash when disinflation begins. The rotation out of energy and into growth is fast and violent — you need to move quickly when the signal changes.

3. **Ignoring the lag.** The Fed's rate hikes impact inflation with a 12–18 month lag. When the Fed hiked aggressively in 2022, the inflation effect arrived in mid-2023. Don't expect immediate regime transitions after policy changes.

4. **Confusing CPI with PCE.** The Fed officially targets PCE, not CPI. PCE inflation runs roughly 0.3–0.5% lower than CPI. When the Fed says "2% inflation target," they mean 2% PCE — equivalent to ~2.3–2.5% CPI. Regime threshold adjustments: use 2.8% PCE as the equivalent of 3.0% CPI.
