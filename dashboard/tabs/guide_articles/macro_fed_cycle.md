## Macro Fed Cycle Strategy

**In plain English:** The Federal Reserve's interest rate cycle — hiking, pausing, cutting — is one of the most powerful forces in equity markets. Different phases of the Fed cycle produce dramatically different return profiles across sectors, factor exposures, and volatility regimes. This strategy systematically adjusts portfolio positioning based on where we are in the Fed cycle, using FOMC language, dot plot changes, and rate futures as inputs.

---

### The Four Phases of the Fed Cycle

| Phase | Description | Typical Duration | SPY Avg Return |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Hiking aggressively | FFR rising rapidly (50–75 bps/meeting) | 6–18 months | −5% to −15% |
| 2. Hiking slowing / pause | Final hikes, "higher for longer" talk | 3–9 months | 0% to +8% |
| 3. Pivoting / first cut | First rate cut (dovish pivot) | 1–3 months | +5% to +15% |
| 4. Cutting cycle | Consecutive cuts | 6–24 months | Highly variable |

The Phase 3 pivot is typically the best single entry point in equities. Markets often front-run it by 3–6 months.

---

### How to Identify the Phase

**Input 1: Fed Funds Futures (CME FedWatch)**
- Implied rate at next 3 meetings tells you market expectation
- Shift from "pricing hikes" to "pricing holds" = Phase 2
- Shift from "pricing holds" to "pricing cuts" = transition to Phase 3

**Input 2: FOMC Statement Language**
- "Ongoing increases" → Phase 1
- "Some additional firming may be appropriate" → Phase 2 start
- "Patient" + no forward guidance → Phase 2 deep
- "Any firming in policy" removed → Phase 3 approaching

**Input 3: Dot Plot Median**
- Rising dot plot = Phase 1
- Stable/falling dot plot = Phase 2–3

### Real Trade Walkthrough

> **Date:** November 1, 2023 · FOMC meeting. Rates held at 5.25–5.50%. Powell says "process of getting inflation to 2% has a long way to go." Market interprets as hawkish Phase 2.

**Positioning through late 2023 (Phase 2 — pause):**
- Equity weight: 75% (neutral, not max risk)
- Sector tilts: overweight energy (XLE), healthcare (XLV), financials (XLF) — sectors that do well in high-rate stable environment
- Bond allocation: heavy short-duration (SHY, T-bills) — earn 5%+ with no duration risk
- Options: sell covered calls on SPY position (high IV from rate uncertainty)

**December 13, 2023 FOMC:** Dot plot shows THREE cuts expected in 2024. Language shifts dramatically dovish. **The pivot signal.**

**Immediate repositioning (Phase 3 protocol):**
- Increase equity to 90%
- Rotate into rate-sensitive sectors: REITs (VNQ), utilities (XLU), small caps (IWM)
- Extend bond duration: move from SHY to IEF (7–10 year)
- Buy IWM calls: March 2024 $190 calls at $4.80 (small caps rally hard in rate cuts)

**Outcome through March 2024:**
- SPY: +11.2%
- IWM: +8.7% (small caps underperformed — typical, pivot front-running was messy)
- IWM $190 calls: expired worthless (IWM peaked at $205 then fell back) — call premium lost
- Overall portfolio: +9.8% vs SPY +11.2% (slight underperformance due to sector tilts)

### Phase-Specific Playbooks

**Phase 1 (Aggressive hiking):**
- Underweight long-duration assets (TLT, growth stocks, REITs)
- Overweight energy, commodities, short-duration fixed income
- Sell OTM calls on equity positions (elevated IV)
- Hedge with SPY put spreads

**Phase 2 (Pause / higher for longer):**
- Neutral equity weight
- Heavy short-duration bond allocation (earn risk-free 4–5%)
- Sell covered calls on SPY
- Avoid highly leveraged companies (they suffer most from sustained high rates)

**Phase 3 (Pivot signal — first cut):**
- Increase equity aggressively
- Rotate into rate-sensitive: small caps (IWM), REITs (VNQ), utilities (XLU), long bonds (TLT)
- Buy 3–6 month upside calls on IWM and XLU
- Close short-duration bond positions; extend duration

**Phase 4 (Cutting cycle):**
- Maximum equity weight
- Long-duration bonds complement equities well IF cutting for "soft landing"
- BUT: if cutting due to recession, equity allocation should be reduced simultaneously
- Monitor unemployment data to distinguish "insurance cuts" from "panic cuts"

---

### The Pivot Trade Warning

The Phase 3 pivot is the most exciting but most dangerous moment:

**Scenario A (Soft landing cuts):** Economy slows mildly, Fed cuts preventatively → stocks rally hard, bonds rally → the best of all worlds

**Scenario B (Recession cuts):** Economy enters recession, Fed cuts to stimulate → initial stock rally, then collapse as earnings disappoint → the trap

The key differentiation: **unemployment rate**. If unemployment is rising while cuts begin, it's Scenario B — the cut is chasing the recession, not preventing it. If unemployment is stable or falling, it's Scenario A.

The 2019 "mid-cycle adjustment" cuts (3 cuts) were Scenario A — the economy re-accelerated. The 2007–2008 emergency cuts were Scenario B — disastrous for equities despite the cuts.

### Entry Checklist

- [ ] Check CME FedWatch for probability distribution of next 3 FOMC meetings
- [ ] Read most recent FOMC statement for language shifts
- [ ] Compare current dot plot to previous dot plot — did the median 2024 or 2025 dot fall?
- [ ] Identify current phase and check sector rotation implications
- [ ] Rebalance in 2–3 stages, not all at once

---

### Common Mistakes

1. **Trading the first hint of a pivot.** Powell says "discussing slowing the pace" — that's not Phase 3. Phase 3 is confirmed only when the dot plot shows cuts AND the statement removes "any additional firming."

2. **Ignoring the "landing" outcome.** The Fed cycle phase tells you rate direction but not the economic outcome. Always overlay the unemployment/recession signal to determine if cuts are good (soft landing) or dangerous (recession).

3. **Over-rotating into rate-sensitive sectors on Phase 3 signal.** Yes, REITs and utilities re-rate up on rate cuts. But if you concentrate 40% of portfolio in XLU and VNQ waiting for the rate cut that's 6 months away, you suffer significant opportunity cost and duration risk in the meantime.
