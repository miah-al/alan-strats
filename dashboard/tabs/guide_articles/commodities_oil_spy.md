## Commodities — Oil / SPY Rotation

**In plain English:** Oil prices and stock market returns have a complex, regime-dependent relationship. In "growth boom" regimes, both rise together (economic expansion drives demand for both). In "supply shock" regimes (e.g., 1973, 2022), oil spikes while stocks crash. This strategy monitors the oil-equity relationship to identify which regime is active and rotates between XLE (energy equity), SPY (broad market), and USO/UCO (oil exposure) accordingly.

---

### Oil-SPY Relationship: The Four Regimes

| Oil Move | Economy | Cause | Stock Market Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Oil rising moderately (≤ +2%/month) | Expanding | Demand-pull | SPY rises too (+0.8 correlation) |
| Oil rising sharply (> +5%/month) | Slowing | Supply-shock | SPY falls (−0.3 correlation) |
| Oil falling moderately | Slowing | Demand weakness | SPY falls (risk-off) |
| Oil falling sharply | May stabilize | Supply glut / recession end | SPY can rally (input cost relief) |

The key distinction: **demand-driven oil rise = good for stocks; supply-driven oil rise = stagflation = bad for stocks.**

---

### Identifying the Oil Regime

**Signals for demand-driven oil rise (bullish for stocks):**
- Global manufacturing PMI > 52 (expanding)
- IEA demand forecast revisions upward
- Emerging market growth acceleration (China PMI > 52)
- Oil rise is gradual (not a sudden gap)

**Signals for supply-driven oil spike (bearish for stocks):**
- Sudden OPEC production cut announcement
- Middle East geopolitical event (Strait of Hormuz, Houthis, pipeline attacks)
- Oil spikes > 10% in < 2 weeks
- US CPI energy component diverges upward from core CPI

### Real Trade Walkthrough: 2022 Supply Shock

> **February 24, 2022:** Russia invades Ukraine. WTI crude oil: $97 → jumps to $130 within 3 weeks (supply shock signal — sudden, geopolitical).

**Day 1 (Feb 25, 2022):**
- Regime signal: supply shock (sudden geopolitical spike)
- SPY at $428. Expected: SPY will underperform significantly while energy outperforms.

**Trade:**
- Sell 20% of SPY position (reduces portfolio risk)
- Buy XLE (energy equity) → $50,000 at $74.50
- Buy UCO (leveraged oil ETF, 2×) → $10,000 at $22.80

**March 2022:**
- WTI oil peaks at $130 on March 7 (+34% from invasion)
- XLE: $80.20 (+7.7% from entry)
- UCO: $28.40 (+24.6% from entry — 2× leveraged)
- SPY: $424 (−1% — held up briefly on Fed expectations)

**March 14, 2022:** Regime monitor shows oil reversing (diplomacy talks → supply recovery thesis). Signal shifts.

**Exit:**
- Sell XLE at $80.20 → +$3,860 profit
- Sell UCO at $28.40 → +$2,460 profit
- Restore SPY position at $424 (slightly below Feb 25 sell price)
- **Net gain: ~$6,320** from the rotation trade

**For contrast — 2023 demand-driven scenario:**
- Oil rising on China reopening demand (Jan–April 2023)
- Both XLE and SPY rose together — no rotation needed
- Regime monitor: demand-driven → stay in SPY, no overweight XLE needed

### The XLE vs USO Trade-off

| | XLE (Energy Stocks) | USO (Oil Futures) |
|---|---|---|
| Correlation to oil | 0.65–0.75 | 0.90–0.95 |
| Dividend | ~3.5% yield | 0% |
| Contango drag | None | 1–3%/year (futures roll cost) |
| Volatility | Moderate | High |
| Best for | 3–12 month hold | Days–weeks tactical |

For most strategies, XLE is better for holding 1–3 months. USO/UCO only for very short-term (< 3 weeks) tactical oil exposure.

---

### Oil as Inflation Predictor

CPI energy components lead Core CPI by ~2 months:
- Oil +30% → Energy CPI: +30% 1 month later → Core CPI: +0.5–0.8% 2 months later

**Trading implication:** Oil spike in January → expect Core CPI print in March to disappoint (higher than expected) → SPY falls on CPI day → pre-position SPY put before March CPI release.

**Historical accuracy (2010–2024):**
- When oil > 25% 3-month return AND 3m/10Y spread > 0: CPI miss (actual > expected) 71% of next 2 monthly CPI readings
- When oil < −20% 3-month return: CPI beat (actual < expected) 65% of next 2 monthly CPI readings

### Entry Checklist

- [ ] Determine oil regime: demand-driven or supply-driven (using speed of move + fundamental cause)
- [ ] Supply shock: reduce SPY, add XLE; duration trade 2–8 weeks
- [ ] Demand-driven rise: no rotation needed; SPY and XLE both perform
- [ ] Oil falling with recession signal: reduce both SPY and XLE; go defensive (XLP, XLV, SHY)
- [ ] Use XLE for multi-week holds; UCO only for <2 week tactical plays

### Common Mistakes

1. **Chasing oil after a 30% move.** The best rotation trade is entered early in the supply shock, not after oil has already moved. By the time oil is at $130 and CNBC is reporting "oil will go to $200," the supply shock is usually nearly over.

2. **Ignoring contango in oil futures ETFs.** USO (United States Oil Fund) rolls its futures monthly and consistently loses 1–3% per year to contango (when futures are priced higher than spot). It's a terrible long-term hold but acceptable for 1–3 week trades during acute supply shocks.

3. **Confusing "oil companies do well when oil is high" with "buy oil stocks in all supply shocks."** In the 2020 COVID crash, oil went negative (demand collapse) while XLE fell 60%. Both oil and energy stocks can crash together if the oil decline is demand-driven rather than supply-driven.

4. **Not position-sizing based on geopolitical uncertainty.** In a war-related supply shock, the situation can reverse instantly (ceasefire, OPEC emergency production increase). Keep oil trade positions at 5–10% of portfolio maximum, not 30–40%, even if you have high conviction.
